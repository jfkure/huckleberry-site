Standard headphone-style mic jack accepts a clip-on lavalier mic, letting you position the sensor right at the muzzle or on your body — not just wherever the box sits.

Records up to 60 shots across 6 review screens. Press Stop to scroll through splits, total times per shot, and battery level. Everything you need, nothing you don't.

Full status indicator — solid white during the 5-second countdown after pressing Start, blinks continuously while actively listening for shots, then turns off when you press Stop. Also stays solid in the max sound and threshold screens. Bright enough to illuminate your bag when digging for gear.

Latching power button lights up blue the moment the timer is on — clear status at a glance, even in bright daylight. The enclosure has a raised guard structure around the button to keep it from getting bumped on in your range bag or pocket. Press once to power on, depress to power off.

03 — How It Works
Simple.
Fast. Done.

Two Buttons.
Zero Learning Curve.

Green = Start. Press it to immediately begin recording at the default threshold of 150 — tested and working with AR-15 and pistol. Hold it to view the live mic peak level screen — see exactly how loud your shots register. Press Stop to exit that screen.

Red = Stop. Hold it to enter the threshold setting screen — choose your sensitivity from 0–500 in steps of 10. Press Stop again to save and exit. After a timed run, press Stop to scroll through results — split times, total times, battery level — one screen at a time. Up to 60 shots across 6 screens.

When you're ready to run, press Start. The buzzer fires after a 5-second delay. Draw, engage, and let the MK1 do the rest.
